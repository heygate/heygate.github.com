Southwark Council instigated eviction proceedings against 198 households on the Heygate estate, having failed to build the new homes originally promised to residents. Despite this, Southwark's website claims that "residents have been rehoused around the borough in new homes of their choice." In August 2012, it issued a press statement claiming that "The majority of people rehoused are delighted with their new housing, and are pleased to have left the Heygate after over 70% of residents voted for its demolition in August 1999." A group of Heygate residents past and present have come together in response to these inaccurate claims. We feel insulted by the way events are being misrepresented, and are attempting to set the record straight. We are calling for other former residents to contribute either by sending us a photograph and written account of their thoughts concerning the regeneration, or by participating in a filmed interview. Please contact us at heygateresidents@gmail.com
