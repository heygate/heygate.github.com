Human Dustbin?

Despite Met Police statistics showing a crime rate 45% below the borough average, the council claims in press releases that the Heygate estate suffered from crime and antisocial behaviour. Councillor Colley has described it as a 'concrete fortress' and a 'blight on the landscape'. In press interviews, Council leader Peter John has described the estate as "a by-word for social failure, for crime and anti-social behaviour".
As a result, the estate has gained an undeserved reputation: being labelled "Muggers' paradise" in sensationalist press articles by the BBC and the Evening Standard, and a "human dustbin" where people "live like pigs" in the Daily Telegraph. Council officials have also claimed that Heygate residents have been rehoused in new homes at the Elephant and are delighted with them.

We feel insulted by the way we are being misrepresented, and a number of us former Heygate residents have gotten together in an attempt to set the record straight. We are calling for other former residents to contribute either by sending us a photograph and written account of their experiences, or by participating in a filmed interview. Please contact us at heygateresidents@gmail.com if you would like to contribute.
